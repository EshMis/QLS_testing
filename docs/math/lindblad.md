# Lindbladian NDME simulation of the enzyme ODE

The primary reference is the archived paper
[Shang et al., *Designing a Nearly Optimal Quantum Algorithm for Linear Differential Equations via Lindbladians*](../references/Lindbladians_reference.pdf),
Phys. Rev. Lett. **135**, 120604 (2025). This branch implements its Eqs. (2)--(5),
not a generic population-decay analogy. It remains separate from QLS.

## From a linear ODE to a Lindbladian

The paper writes the homogeneous ODE as

$$
\dot\mu(t)=-V\mu(t).
$$

For this repository's lifted equation $\dot\mu=A\mu$, set $V=-A$ and

$$
B=\frac{V+V^\dagger}{2},\qquad
H_1=\frac{V-V^\dagger}{2i}.
$$

The direct construction requires the semidissipative condition $B\succeq0$.
The order-1 enzyme operator is not semidissipative, so the implementation uses
the smallest uniform shift

$$
\kappa=\max(0,-\lambda_{\min}(B)+\epsilon),\qquad B'=B+\kappa I,
$$

and forms $G=\sqrt{2B'}$. The encoded dynamics is generated by

$$
-iH_1-\frac12G^\dagger G=A-\kappa I.
$$

Multiplication by $e^{\kappa t}$ during extraction recovers the unshifted ODE.

## Nondiagonal density-matrix encoding

Normalize $u_0=\mu_0/\|\mu_0\|$ and initialize

$$
\rho_0=|+\rangle\!\langle+|\otimes|u_0\rangle\!\langle u_0|
=\frac12\begin{bmatrix}\sigma_0&\sigma_0\\\sigma_0&\sigma_0\end{bmatrix}.
$$

On the ancilla-extended space use

$$
H=\begin{bmatrix}H_1&0\\0&0\end{bmatrix},\qquad
F=\begin{bmatrix}G&0\\0&0\end{bmatrix},
$$

inside the GKSL equation

$$
\dot\rho=-i[H,\rho]+F\rho F^\dagger
-\frac12\{F^\dagger F,\rho\}.
$$

The upper-right block evolves as

$$
\rho_{01}(t)=\frac12e^{(A-\kappa I)t}|u_0\rangle\langle u_0|,
$$

so the classical verification readout is

$$
\mu(t)=2\|\mu_0\|e^{\kappa t}\rho_{01}(t)|u_0\rangle.
$$

Hardware would measure observables or use the paper's amplification procedure;
full amplitude extraction here is solely for simulator verification.

## Enzyme reduction and verification

The actual mass-action variables are

$$
(S,X_1,X_2,X_3,P,C_1,C_2,C_3,C_4).
$$

The interactive NDME mode uses Carleman order 1: ODE dimension 9, density
dimension 18, and Liouville dimension 324. Order 2 would lift to 54 ODE
coordinates and a 108-dimensional density matrix, which is inappropriate for a
responsive dashboard. The canonical reference is the exact sparse Liouvillian
exponential followed by the same off-diagonal extraction map. It is generated
outside both execution paths, so switching classical/PennyLane evolution cannot
change the plotted truth.

The UI maps outputs to separate `S`, `Xs=(X1,X2,X3)`, `P`, and
`Cs=(C1,C2,C3,C4)` comparisons. Diagnostics include extraction error, trace
defect, minimum density eigenvalue, shift, jump rank, and generator identity
error.

## Paper complexity

Writing $\eta_T=\|\mu(T)\|$, $\alpha_V\ge\|V\|$, precision $\epsilon$,
and $\Delta$ for the smallest nonzero eigenvalue of $B$, the paper gives for
time-independent dynamics the direct-access query scaling

$$
\widetilde O\!\left(
\eta_T^{-1}\Delta^{-1}\alpha_V^2T
\frac{\log^2(1/\epsilon)}{\log\log(1/\epsilon)}
\right),
$$

while square-root access to $H_1$ and $G_i$ gives

$$
\widetilde O\!\left(
\eta_T^{-1}\alpha_VT
\frac{\log(1/\epsilon)}{\log\log(1/\epsilon)}
\right).
$$

These are oracle-model estimates, not the runtime of the dense verification
code. The shift may make recovery ill-conditioned through $e^{\kappa T}$.

## PennyLane short-time channel path

`lindblad_practice_pennylane` uses a four-coordinate enzyme-like chain
$(S,X_1,P,C_1)$, producing an eight-dimensional NDME density matrix on three
qubits. `lindblad_enzyme_ndme_pennylane` applies the same construction to all
nine enzyme coordinates and is intended for short order-1 demonstrations.

For substep $\delta t$, the implementation starts from

$$
K_0=I-\delta t\left(iH+\frac12\sum_jL_j^\dagger L_j\right),
\qquad K_j=\sqrt{\delta t}L_j.
$$

If $S_K=\sum_jK_j^\dagger K_j$, each operator is replaced by
$K_jS_K^{-1/2}$. The resulting finite-step channel is exactly completely
positive and trace preserving, while approximating the target GKSL generator
to first order. PennyLane `QubitChannel` applies it on `default.mixed`.

The density matrix therefore evolves through a PennyLane mixed-state QNode at
every substep. This is not yet a hardware-native channel decomposition: a device
requires matching native noise or a Stinespring dilation with environment
qubits. Channel-discretization, shift-amplified extraction, trace, positivity,
and observable errors are measured against the independent ground truth.
